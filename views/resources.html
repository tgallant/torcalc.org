Bandwidth Allocation Resources

There seems to be a split mind in regards to bandwidth allocation for tor relays. Some say it is best to ration your bandwidth throughout the month and others say it is best to use up your bandwidth as fast as you can and hibernate until your data usage is reset.

The main argument against hibernation is that the bandwidth authorities will rate your relay lower if it isn't consistently pushing traffic. This is not the case, however. A known relay with higher advertised bandwidth will get more traffic than a more consistent relay with lower bandwidth. On top of that, adding relays with adjusted bandwidth rates can slow the rest of the network down. Even with a moderate amount of available throughput the adjusted bandwidth rates are very slow (500GB/mo translates to 190KB/s).

The following are some links collected from the tor mailing list and the tor wiki that discuss these matters in great detail.
